A no phone challenge we should all try!

Posted on: 08/12/18

I was watching a video on YouTube in which a girl challenged herself to not use her phone an hour before bed and in the morning when she wakes up for a week long. It seemed impossible to achieve but at the end of the week she said she felt way better so I thought I want to give it a shot as well.

The reason behind this challenge lies in how we start and end each day. Being on my phone, scrolling through pointless stuff is always the last thing I do before I fall asleep and I am pretty sure that most people are like that, as well. But doing that, especially being on social media, right before we go to bed or when we wake up puts pressure on us and on our brains that we shouldn’t deal with.

The first couple of days, I found it very hard to resist the urge to grab my phone and just stay on it for a while. It is unbelievable how weird I felt about it and how attached I found myself to be. Of course, I still used my phone to make necessary phone calls, quickly answer messages etc. and many of us would not be available to avoid that and that’s understandable. The point here is that we don’t go on social media and pointlessly scroll through.

By the end of the week, it was a bit easier to keep up with than the first couple of days but I still can’t say that I got used to it. I didn’t continue with this challenge but what I decided is that I would some days still do that if I feel like I need to as it felt very recharging in a sense that not being on my phone for these two important hours of the day allowed me to feel less ‘weighted’ with random, unnecessary and toxic matters. I would describe these phone-free hours as mind detoxers that I strongly recommend and think everyone needs at least once in a while!
