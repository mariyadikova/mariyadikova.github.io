Overthinking, stress & meditation

Posted on: 06/02/19

Of course, being in your final year of university is going to cause stress for anybody. By no means I will be trying to complain in this article, but simply share a bit of my experience. For the past year, my stress level has definitely reached its highest point yet. Yes, there are far more stressful and worrying situations in life but approaching this final barrier between being a young adult and actually having to be a proper adult with all of its advantages, disadvantages, responsibilities and so on, could be scary and I believe that many people would agree with me.

And yes, everyone has a different experience that might not quite match what I am saying but at the end of the day all of the university, deadlines, graduation, career and general future stress and overthinking that I have experienced is just an example of such a period in one’s life and the examples I will be giving for trying to deal with this period could easily be ‘applied’ to many other situations.

One of the things I tried a few months ago when I was approaching assignments and deadlines and therefore experienced a period of identical, busy everyday routine which didn’t let me fall asleep easily at night, was using a meditation app. You might have heard of Headspace. I gave it a go one night and I really liked it and found it quite calming. The sound of this soothing voice really helped with calming my mind and body. It’s a bit like ASMR. I used the app a few times after this but then got a bit bored with it and deleted it. It sure is a short-term solution you could try, though.

Taking regular walks alone also helps. In my case, I also take the opportunity to take my dog out for a walk, so I am not quite alone but I don’t like just walking around fully on my own, anyway. It helps to clear your mind, think fresh and maybe come up with a fresh solution or an approach to your problem.

Just sitting down in a room by yourself, and trying to think about your problem without panicking, on the contrary - trying to calm yourself down, is always a nice thing to do for yourself. I strongly recommend dedicating a special amount of time to be doing this and actively thinking about something that worries you instead of it being in the back of your mind for the whole day and exhausting you mentally, as well as physically. Your little alone ‘session’ might last for as long as you want it to, it doesn’t matter if it is for 10 minutes or even 10 seconds, as long as at the end of it you’ve adopted a motivational approach that you will be okay and can do anything.
