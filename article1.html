Three things that I have found to worsen my sleep

Posted on: 17/12/18

Me and a healthy sleeping cycle were never friends in the first place. I either sleep so much that I feel even more exhausted during the rest of the day or too little - which is fine at first until it has happened for a few consecutive days. Finding the right balance is not an easy task but there are a few things that we could all gain from, starting with leaving yourself enough hours of sleep. Some of us think that 5/6 hours of sleep each night is enough to feel okay and keep you going throughout the day but it is scientifically proven otherwise. 7/8 hours of sleep is what we should be getting daily. Not much more but definitely not any less.

Having a few coffees throughout the day might come with its short-term benefits but I have definitely come to the conclusion that more than two coffees per day is a no-go for me. Everyone is different and you may find that three or four coffees for you don’t affect your sleep, but if you are someone who drinks lots of coffee and can’t get enough of sleep or is often feeling tired, even when you have just woken up, think about your coffee consumption and try to figure out if less would end up being more for you.

I don’t usually do work late in the evening unless I have things to catch up on or I’m close to uni deadlines. However, when I do, it makes it even harder for me to fall asleep afterwards. So if you are someone who works late in bed on your laptop or whatever device or just in general, you should do it as rarely as you can. It can be very mentally and therefore also physically exhausting to be thinking about work right before bed instead of relaxing your body and brain which are at their most vulnerable state at this hour.
