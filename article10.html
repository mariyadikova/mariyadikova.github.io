Is it my fault?

Posted on: 16/01/19

Recently in my life I have lost people that I used to be very close with. I lost them in a sense that we no longer have the relationship we used to have. One of those people is my brother and the other was my best friend. Maybe you’re asking yourself “but how can you lose your own brother?” but I don’t know the answer to that question either.

Both of these people just walked out of my life, unexpectedly and inexplicably. It’s not like we had an argument or we experienced a certain situation that made us disconnect straight after. To this day, I still don’t know how to explain many things to myself and when I start thinking about it I feel confused and heartbroken. I have asked myself “is it my fault?” a thousand times and quite often I answered myself differently. And yes, of course, there is a background story behind each of the two cases but recently I’ve started to realise that maybe I haven’t done enough to get these people back in my life but I definitely haven’t done anything for them to decide to walk out of it in the first place.

From blaming myself, I started gaining awareness and changing my view on these situations. I could also say that they have helped in my process of growing as an individual and an adult, although I would have never thought before that my own brother is going to be someone to actively ‘encourage’ this process in such a way. At the end of the day, family is family, and my door would always stay open. In the meantime, I learned many things – about myself, about others and about life in general. Some things in life are meant to happen, some things happen for a reason and many times they are not what we would expect or like but it is how we grow. I think it is important to once in a while think about all the things and people we have in our lives and focus on that, and be thankful.
